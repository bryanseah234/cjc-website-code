Sports Carnival 2017

Text By: Charlotte Wu (2T06)



On the 20 May 2017, Catholic Junior College held its annual Sports and Aesthetics Carnival. With the Mid-Year Examinations just over and the school term drawing to a close, this Sports Carnival was a much-welcomed respite from the stressful school curriculum, and a platform for students to interact with their peers out of the classroom whilst fostering sportsmanship.

Gathered at the grandstand bright and early, students were addressed by the emcees for the day: Mugdha Jaruhar (1T03), Clement Yeo (2T06), Dylan Sim (2T16) and Foo Ji Han (2T03). Following the theme of the Empire Games and the four different factions as represented by the four houses Patrick, Joseph, Albert and Michael, the event was officially opened by the ignition of the flame and the march-in of the Sports Leaders.


House Captains of each of the houses led a round of roaring cheers to hype up the crowd before students were released to their respective events for the day. Sports Leaders acted as referees for each of the many games, such as Touch Rugby, Stick-and-Throw, Basketball, and Netball. With students playing their hearts out and displaying true sportsmanship, the exuberant and energetic atmosphere all around was not dampened in spite of the scorching weather. In the final and most anticipated event, the performers for each house’s dance segment had the audience at rapt attention throughout with their simultaneously enthusiastic and fluid motions set in time with the music.


The Carnival rounded up with a prize-presentation, with Albert House clinching the title of Sports Carnival Champions for the second year running, followed by Patrick, Michael and Joseph House.
