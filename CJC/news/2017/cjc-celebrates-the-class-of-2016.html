CJC Celebrates the Class of 2016




24 February 2017

CJC Celebrates the Class of 2016

The Class of 2016 has done the college proud in achieving commendable results in their performance at the GCE ‘A’ Level Examinations. Their tenacity and resilience in overcoming challenges to achieve their goals are true to the spirit of a Thinker with a Mission and Leader with a Heart.

93.8% of students have attained at least 3 H2 passes and a pass in GP / Knowledge and Inquiry. The Class of 2016’s commendable performance is also illustrated by pass rates that equaled or exceeded the national average in the following H2 and H1 subjects: H2 Chemistry, H2 Biology, H2 Physics, H2 English Language and Linguistics, H2 Geography, H2 History, H2 Economics, H2 Mathematics, H2 Literature in English, H2 Knowledge and Inquiry, H1 Literature in English, H1 General Studies in Chinese, H1 Geography, H1 History, H1 Economics, H1 Physics, H1 Chemistry, and H1 Biology.

The following subjects achieved improved distinction rates as compared to 2015: H2 Physics, H2 Biology, H2 English Language and Linguistics, H2 Mathematics, H2 Literature in English, H1 Chemistry, H1 Mathematics, H1 History, H1 Geography, H1 General Studies in Chinese, H1 Literature in English, H1 Chinese Language, H1 Malay Language, and H1 Tamil Language.

The following subjects achieved improved pass rates as compared to 2015: H2 Physics, H2 Chemistry, H2 Biology, H2 English Language and Linguistics, H2 History, H2 Economics, H2 Mathematics, H2 Literature in English, H2 Knowledge & Inquiry, H1 General Studies in Chinese, H1 Literature in English, H1 Geography, H1 History, H1 Biology, H1 Physics, H1 Chemistry and H1 Chinese Language (B), and H1 Tamil Language (B).

The following subjects achieved improved Mean Subject Grade as compared to 2015: H2 Physics, H2 Chemistry, H2 Biology, H2 Literature in English, H2 Mathematics, H1 Mathematics, H1 Physics, H1 Chemistry, H1 Biology, H1 History, H1 Geography, H1 Literature in English, and H1 Economics.

The following subjects achieved a 100% pass rate: H2 Physics, H2 Biology, H2 History, H2 English Language and Linguistics, H2 Literature in English, H2 Knowledge and Inquiry, H1 History, H1 Chemistry, H1 Biology, H1 Literature in English, H1 General Studies in Chinese, H1 Chinese Language (B), and H1 Tamil Language (B).

Beyond his or her grades, a true CJCian embodies the traits of a thinker and a leader centred on values. The college would like to celebrate the academic and holistic achievements of the following outstanding CJCians:

Nicholas Choong

Nicholas was an independent and motivated learner who maintained high standards in all his work. He was awarded the Flame Scholarship in recognition of his outstanding performance. He clinched the Bronze medal at the National ‘A’ Division competitions in Fencing, and was also a recipient of the Eagles Award and Sports Colours Award. His stellar leadership abilities are evident in his role as a National Fencer, Sports Leader and the Vice-Captain of Albert House. Nicholas was the main emcee at the Inter-School Golf Championships in 2016 and the opening ceremony of the Inter-School Games in 2016. He took a keen interest in developing his juniors and in imparting valuable leadership lessons to them as an outgoing House Leader. 

“One great thing about CJC is that it has provided me with a holistic education. It is an institution that imbues true values to their students. The CJ ethos is about being a better person and learning to appreciate those who have helped you. It was the community that guided and motivated me to achieve greater heights.”

Tierney Lee 
Tierney exudes the qualities of a Thinker with a Mission and Leader with a Heart. His openness to fresh and new ideas, as well as his enthusiasm for learning have led to commendable academic achievements. A self-driven individual, Tierney managed to excel in whatever he put his mind to. He seized opportunities for personal growth by pursuing the Work Attachment and Job Shadowing Programme and the Harvard Model Congress Asia (2016). To stretch his leadership potential, Tierney developed himself through his role as a Student Councillor. His designation in the Community Involvement Wing allowed him to serve as the voice of the student body, ensuring the smooth running and operating of the Wing’s various responsibilities. His commitment to service has enabled him to be an effective leader with a deep sense of mission. 

“I believe that as a leader, it’s all about the initiative and ability to get the team together and agree on something. As student councillors, we had to work very hard together as a team to push programmes and events forward.”

Megan Morais 
Megan was an awardee of MOE’s English Language Elective Programme Scholarship. She is an intellectually curious student who never ceased to make connections between what she learnt in the classroom and her personal experiences. It is this trait which motivated Megan to participate in various conferences and competitions. She was awarded the Best Position Paper at the Dunman High ASEAN Plus Summit and Honourable Mention at the River Valley Model United Nations Conference. As Vice-President of the Political Science Society, Megan exhibited moral courage and dared to speak up against objectionable behaviour. Her passion to serve also motivated Megan to volunteer to be in-charge of the Games Committee for her class’ Local Service Learning Project (LSLP) for the wards of Boys’ Town, and to become an agent of change and advocacy. 

“The teachers believed in my potential to do well and that helped so much. Their faith in me was important in reinforcing my sense of duty and loyalty to my purpose of being a student leader.”

James Chua Zhi Loon 
James was consistently placed among the top 10% of his cohort in all major school examinations. A member of the Mathematics Society, James commendably took charge of both the publicity and logistical aspects of the Amathzing Race for secondary school student participants as part of the Organising Committee for the Math and Sciensational Week in both 2015 and 2016. James’ avid interest in computational and technical skills also led him to take up a one-month course, entitled “Let’s Code”, on the Ruby Programming language at the Singapore Management University. James truly inspires with his voracity for learning and sincere dedication to excellence. 

“In CJ, Circle Time during Student Development (SD) lessons were important in teaching us perseverance and moral values. I learnt that if I really paid attention to who I am at heart, it can help me to overcome challenges.”

Benjamin Liow 

Benjamin demonstrated a zest for life and a passion for learning through his participation in activities and programmes that extended beyond the formal curriculum. As a participant in the Harvard Model Congress Asia (HMCA), he was able to explore alternative histories, which enriched his study of History, and for which he was given Honourable Mention. He was committed to serving the community in a local Service Learning Project (SLP) and even joined a mission trip to Cambodia on his own accord. His zeal to experience different aspects of life also led him to participate fully in activities such as The Straits Times Forum in 2016, Outward Bound Taiwan, the College’s 40th Anniversary Carnival, and National Day Fundraiser in 2016.


“For me, it simply boils down to this: for every action that you do in your life, there has to be some kind of meaning to it. When you’re a leader, all your actions have to amount to the mission you are living out in order to make the world a better place for everyone.”


Claudia Lee JIa Min 
With a keen interest in law and debate, Claudia strove to extend her knowledge beyond the classroom. She was able to voice her opinions and presented effective arguments which won the respect of her peers. She also participated in the Literature and Linguistics Module in the Engaging Students Partnering Education (ESPE) 2016 conference, held at and by the National University of Singapore and achieved third placing overall in the SG50 Entrepreneurs Trail. Claudia was active in developing herself as a leader, where she assumed the role of Vice-President of Nexus. 

“The leadership position I held in my CCA gave me many opportunities to step up and allowed me to gain confidence in the way I speak. There were many times where I was required to speak out so it gave me the confidence to step up, speak out and lead.”

Kelley Megan Tan 
Kelley demonstrated enthusiasm and a great capacity for learning. She embodied the pursuit for Truth and Love – courageous in her pursuit of learning and in maintaining standards in her academic work and in her CCA. As President of the English Drama Society, she guided and led her members in the Singapore Youth Festival performance of “Aftershock”. She believed in stepping out of her comfort zone to take on other roles such as being the student host for the college’s Think Career initiative. In addition, she was committed to helping the less privileged through the many hours of service in internal and external local Service Learning Programmes (SLP). 

“Throughout my CJ journey, one thing that I have truly learnt is to remember that my words and actions as a leader has an impact on each and every individual. To be a leader with a heart, we must be aware of everyone around us.”

Timothy Lim 
Timothy embodied the traits of a resilient learner and dependable leader. As Captain of the Rugby team, Timothy was active in developing himself as a leader and modelled the values of tenacity and commitment. He led the team to victory in the Plate Finals in 2016. A firm believer of servant leadership, Timothy also participated in local community projects. These experiences are testament to his holistic development.

“I received a lot of motivation from my teachers. Witnessing their commitment and passion for imparting  knowledge really kept me going in hard times. A lot of motivation also came from my classmates because everyone really put their best foot forward for the ‘A’ level examinations.”

Teddy Zhuang Zheng 

Teddy embodied a thinker and leader who was widely respected by his peers. As the Vice-Captain of the Volleyball team, he modelled resilience and determination. He was active in developing himself as a leader and completed the Outward Bound programme in Hong Kong. Teddy believed in the importance of service and was committed to help the less privileged when he served in a Service Learning Project at Crossroads Foundation. Teddy’s passion for sports and outdoor activities made him a natural choice as a Sports Leader. His experiences demonstrate his firm belief in developing both the mind and body. 





Jerald Thong 
Jerald is an Archbishop scholar who was determined to excel in his studies and leadership. He consistently performed well academically and showed deep engagement in his learning. Beyond his studies, Jerald assumed the role of Secretary for the Track and Field team and was the Subject Representative for Biology in his class. He believed in stepping out of his comfort zone to add to his experiences in CJ. Jerald participated in a Work Attachment & Shadowing Programme (WASP) internship in 2015 and was involved in a Ministerial Dialogue. His experiences have enabled him to grow holistically as an individual who exhibits the values of a critical learner and thoughtful leader. 

“I learnt from CJ that I first need to know what I want before I commit to an endeavour. In the process, I learnt to be supportive to others and to have gratitude to everyone who has supported me throughout my journey.”

Sandy Yong Shan Yi 

Embodying the qualities of a critical thinker and a leader with a heart, Shan Yi was determined to excel in her studies and seized opportunities to develop her personal growth. She displayed a passion for learning and took the opportunity to join the Ministry of Education Economics Student Symposium. Beyond academic studies, Shan Yi was also the House Secretary of one of four main houses in CJC and played Netball actively. Shan Yi is also steadfast in her belief of stepping outside of her own comfort zone. She served as an Orientation Group Leader (OGL) and took part in the Outward Bound Singapore Adventure Challenge in Taiwan in 2014. Her compassion and deep sense of service to others was evident in how she committed herself to a local and an overseas Service Learning Programme (SLP) in the Philippines. 

“Even though it was not an easy journey, I am glad I was able to prove to myself and others that if I set my heart out to do it, I can.”

Shaun Chung
Shaun was a self-directed learner who enjoyed the challenge of new ideas, approaching them with maturity and a critical mind. His diligence and analytical approach towards his studies helped him to attain academic achievements. He showed care for the wider community his participation in a class-initiated Service learning project to promote social interaction between locals and migrant workers. Shaun’s experiences in CJC have enabled him to grow holistically. 

“I am thankful for the teachers in CJC who were very engaging, kind and helpful. They were always there for my peers and I whenever we needed guidance in our learning.”

Throughout their time in CJC, their academic and non-academic achievements demonstrate their ability to balance their commitments and sense of purpose. The Class of 2016 embodies the CJC vision as a Place of Excellence in Learning and Living, continuing the tradition of excellence in academic and character development. The college is committed to offering diverse opportunities for our students to maximise their holistic potential, to strive for higher standards and to pursue knowledge in the service of others.

Mrs Phyllis Lim, the Principal of CJC, celebrates the growth of the Class of 2016. “Their accomplishments are a testament of their growth mindset and their commitment to serving the college and the wider community meaningfully and responsibly. The college would like to congratulate the students who surmounted the odds to achieve commendable results at the ‘A’ Level examinations. Their hard work and sheer passion for learning has led to success and we give thanks for each and every one of them. We encourage this cohort to stay true to their mission and to constantly seek new opportunities.”

Mr Tan Jek Suan, Vice Principal added, “Our heartiest congratulations to the Class of 2016 on their results. We hope and pray that you continue to embody the CJC spirit of Truth and Love as you soar to greater heights of excellence.”

The college would like to extend its appreciation to the Teachers and Support Staff, the Parent Support Group, the School Management Committee and the Alumni for their generous support and prayers for the students.
